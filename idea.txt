
Idea 1: NextCloud Secure Internal File Sharing
Great for: A highly visual "before and after" demonstration.

Fictitious Company: Apex Legal Partners (A boutique law firm handling sensitive client data).

The Problem: Employees are using unapproved, personal Dropbox accounts to share confidential legal documents because the company’s current legacy Windows file share doesn't support remote access. This risks compliance violations (HIPAA/GDPR) and data leaks.

The Linux Solution: A NextCloud server hosted on an internal Linux machine (Ubuntu/Rocky Linux).

Partial Working Solution (Demo): Show the NextCloud web dashboard, a quick drag-and-drop file upload, and how permissions/activity logs keep data secure.

Competitive Matrix: Compare NextCloud (Open-source Linux) vs. Microsoft SharePoint/OneDrive (High per-user monthly licensing fees).

4-Person Presentation Split (1-1.5 mins each):
Presenter 1 (The Hook & Problem): Introduce the law firm, the security risks of "Shadow IT" (Dropbox), and the executive summary.

Presenter 2 (The Solution & Demo): Introduce NextCloud on Linux. Screen-share the partial working solution to show how easy it is for employees to use.

Presenter 3 (Competitive Matrix & Reliability): Compare Linux/NextCloud costs vs. Microsoft. Explain Linux's famous stability and uptime (Reliability/Availability).

Presenter 4 (Cost Analysis & ROI): Break down the budget (hardware/hosting vs. licensing savings) and give the final call to action for executive approval.
